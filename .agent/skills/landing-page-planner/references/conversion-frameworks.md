# Conversion Frameworks Reference

This document outlines key copywriting frameworks used to structure persuasive content. Use these models when the user requests specific improvements to conversion or when generating landing page copy.

## AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
**Best for:** General landing pages, emails, and ads.
- **Attention:** Hook the reader immediately. (Headline/Hero)
- **Interest:** Engage them with relevant facts/benefits. (Subheads/Intro)
- **Desire:** Make them want the product/service. (Benefits/Social Proof)
- **Action:** Tell them exactly what to do next. (CTA)

## PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution)
**Best for:** High-pain problems, service businesses, SaaS.
- **Problem:** Identify the pain point the user is experiencing.
- **Agitation:** Rub salt in the wound; explain why it’s bad/costly.
- **Solution:** Present your product as the only viable fix.

## BAB (Before, After, Bridge)
**Best for:** Case studies, testimonials, and transformation-focused offers.
- **Before:** Describe the current negative state.
- **After:** Describe the desired positive state.
- **Bridge:** Explain how your product gets them from Before to After.

## FAB (Features, Advantages, Benefits)
**Best for:** Product pages, technical specs.
- **Features:** What the product does (technical).
- **Advantages:** Why that feature is helpful.
- **Benefits:** What the user ultimately gets out of it (emotional/outcome).

## The 4 Ps (Picture, Promise, Prove, Push)
**Best for:** Long-form sales letters and video scripts.
- **Picture:** Paint a vivid scene of the problem or desire.
- **Promise:** Make a bold claim about what you can do.
- **Prove:** Back it up with data, testimonials, case studies.
- **Push:** Ask for the sale.
